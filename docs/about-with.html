About WITH

Women in Technological History.

WITH is a Special Interest Group within SHOT whose membership includes both men and women interested in gender issues. It serves both as a support network for women working within the profession and as an activist community that promotes gender analysis within the history of technology.

Founding vision

As founding member and former SHOT President Daryl Hafter wrote, WITH’s goal is to nurture “the subject and the people.”

Open membership

Membership in WITH is open to all members of SHOT.

Gender analysis

WITH continues to foster scholarship that treats gender as a vital analytical category.

Purpose

The two-fold mission of WITH

WITH was formed to support people in the profession and to strengthen the field intellectually through gender analysis.

Support group

WITH serves as a support group that encourages and energizes women working within the profession.

Activist group

WITH also functions as an activist group seeking to foster and promote gender analysis within the history of technology.

Intellectual community

The organization brings together scholars who are interested in gender issues across different regions, traditions, and methodological approaches.

50th Anniversary

Call for submissions

WITH invites submissions for a proposed special section or supplemental issue of Technology & Culture.

What the issue explores

The issue is dedicated to exploring the roles of women and the analytical category of gender in the history of technology.

Geographic scope

Contributions that move beyond established geographic centers of scholarship are especially encouraged, including work on the Global South, Eastern Europe, Asia, Africa, Latin America, and transnational or comparative perspectives.

Why this matters

Broadening the field

The anniversary call makes clear that WITH is committed not only to women in the profession, but also to reshaping where and how the history of technology is studied.

Beyond U.S. and Western Europe

The call particularly welcomes scholarship addressing contexts beyond the better-established centers of the field.

Global and comparative perspectives

Comparative, transnational, and globally connected histories are explicitly welcomed.

Expanding the archive of scholarship

This anniversary milestone is also an invitation to rethink the field’s geographies, subjects, and categories of analysis.
